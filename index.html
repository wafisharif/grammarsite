Independent vs. Dependent Clauses

Learn the difference, test yourself, build sentences, and stop guessing.

Learn the Core Idea

This is the whole game: an independent clause can stand alone as a complete sentence. A dependent clause cannot.

Independent Clause

Has a subject + verb + complete thought.

Example: The robot finished the race.

This can stand by itself. It makes sense alone.

Dependent Clause

Has a subject + verb, but does not express a complete thought.

Example: Because the robot finished the race

You’re left hanging. Because it finished the race... what happened?

Common Dependent Clause Starters

  • because
  • although
  • since
  • when
  • if
  • while
  • unless
  • after
  • before
  • even though

Quick Test

Ask yourself:

  1. Does it have a subject and verb?
  2. Can it stand alone as a full sentence?

If the answer to #2 is no, it’s dependent.

Examples Side by Side

Independent

  • The dog barked.
  • My teacher laughed at the meme.
  • The engineers tested the bridge.
  • I forgot my homework again.

Dependent

  • When the dog barked
  • Because my teacher laughed at the meme
  • Although the engineers tested the bridge
  • If I forgot my homework again

Comma Rule You Actually Need

If a dependent clause comes before an independent clause, you usually use a comma.

Correct: Because I was tired, I went to sleep.

Also correct: I went to sleep because I was tired.
